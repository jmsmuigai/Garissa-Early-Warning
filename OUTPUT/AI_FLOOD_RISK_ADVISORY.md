# 🌍 DISASTER RISK MANAGEMENT ADVISORY: 2026 SUPER EL NIÑO PREPAREDNESS
**To:** H.E. The Governor, Garissa County  
**From:** National Drought Management Authority (NDMA) & Geospatial Intelligence Unit  
**Date:** 2026-06-14  
**Subject:** Early Warning and Early Action Hydrological Alert — Seven Forks Dams Spillage
**Emergency Operations Command**: emergency@garissa.go.ke

## 1. Executive Summary
Hydrological monitoring reports indicate that **Masinga Dam** is spilling. High volumes are cascading through Kamburu, Gitaru, and Kindaruma dams. **Kiambere Dam is highly likely to spill within 5 days**, which will send an unregulated wave of water downstream into Garissa, Tana River, and Lamu counties. A major flood emergency along the Tana River and seasonal Lagha networks is imminent.

## 2. Infrastructure Vulnerability Statistics
- **🏫 Education Sector**: Out of **267** schools analyzed, **44** are at **High Risk** and **27** are at **Extreme Risk**. These facilities are vulnerable to direct physical damage and isolation.
- **🏥 Health Sector**: Out of **87** clinics, **15** are at **High Risk** (including Mororo Dispensary and Madogo Health Centre). An additional **11** clinics face inundation, which will cut off critical medical access.
- **💧 Water Security**: Out of **247** boreholes, **1** are at **High Risk** and **241** are at **Medium Risk**. Contamination of these primary water sources is a major threat.
- **🎪 Refugee Integration**: Dadaab's **417,767 refugees** are exposed to high cholera risks and road isolation along the Lagh Dera systems, threatening the socio-economic transition of the **Shirika Plan**.

## 3. High-Priority Early Action Recommendations

### 🏫 A. Schools & Education Protection
- **Establish Temporary Learning Spaces (TLS)**: Immediately pre-position tents in safe zones like Garissa Township highlands and Dadaab town.
- **Secure Examinations & Assets**: Direct school boards in High/Extreme risk zones to relocate textbooks, laboratory equipment, and solar batteries to elevated shelves or neighboring safe host schools.
- **Trigger Pre-arranged Early Closures**: Coordinate with the Ministry of Education to close vulnerable boarding facilities before major roads (such as the A3 Garissa-Dadaab highway) are cut off.

### 🏥 B. Health & Waterborne Disease Control
- **Pre-position Cholera Prevention Kits**: Deliver IV fluids, oral rehydration salts, and water purification units to safe health hubs (such as Balambala Sub-County Hospital and Garissa Referral Hospital).
- **Establish Mobile Riverine Clinics**: Deploy high-clearance medical trucks and motorboats to provide emergency care to isolated sub-counties (e.g. Balambala, Fafi, and Ijara).
- **Rift Valley Fever (RVF) Vaccination**: Conduct emergency livestock vaccinations along the Tana River floodplains to prevent mosquito-borne disease transmission.

### 💧 C. Borehole Protection & Safe Water Supply
- **Construct Raised Aprons**: Elevate generator houses and borehole pump seals in High/Extreme risk zones by at least 1.5 meters.
- **Shock Chlorination**: Distribute chlorine water treatment chemicals to all community water pans and boreholes located in the Medium/High risk buffer zones.
